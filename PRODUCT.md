# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Everyday Gmail + Google Calendar users — **non-technical first**. People drowning in email and scheduling who want to get through their inbox and book time fast, without learning a power-user tool. They arrive expecting it to "just work" with no onboarding. They use it on both mobile and desktop, often in short focused bursts between other tasks.

## Product Purpose

SlotNest is a keyboard-first command center for Gmail + Google Calendar, built on Corsair. It reshapes email/calendar workflows around speed and AI assistance: a unified command bar (cmd-K) that takes both discrete actions and natural language; AI triage that sorts mail by action + urgency; pre-drafted replies; and free-slot scheduling that turns "find time with X" or an open email into a real calendar invite. Success = the user clears their inbox and books time in fewer clicks and less time than in native Gmail/Calendar, and never needs a tutorial to do it.

## Brand Personality

Minimal, calm, effortless. Three words: **clear, fast, quiet.** The interface gets out of the way — distraction-free, with obvious default actions and advanced options tucked away. Plain language over jargon. Speed is felt through reduced clicks and keyboard shortcuts, not through a dense, intimidating "power tool" aesthetic. Confidence comes from clarity, not from showing off.

## Anti-references

- **Gmail / Outlook** — cluttered legacy webmail, toolbar soup, nested menus, density that creates anxiety. The thing we're replacing.
- **Generic AI-SaaS** — purple gradients, hero-metric cards, cream/parchment backgrounds, decorative glassmorphism, gradient text.
- **Terminal-native power tools** — monospace-everything, jargon, hidden-knowledge interfaces that punish non-technical users. Keyboard-first must not mean expert-only.

## Design Principles

1. **Intuitive without onboarding.** Every screen must be understandable on first sight. If it needs a tutorial, redesign it.
2. **Reduce clicks, surface shortcuts.** The fastest path is the default path. Keyboard accelerators are visible and discoverable, never required.
3. **Obvious default, hidden advanced.** One clear primary action per screen; complexity is progressive, not front-loaded.
4. **Calm over dense.** Speed comes from clarity and fewer steps, not from cramming the screen. Distraction-free wins.
5. **Consistency is the system.** Uniform spacing, typography, and components across every surface so nothing has to be relearned.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA throughout: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, AA-contrast placeholders. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states (the product is keyboard-first — this is core, not a checkbox). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` on every animation. Mobile and desktop both first-class, with touch targets ≥44px on mobile.
